Introduction

An overview of water contamination and the role of advanced nanomaterials in environmental remediation.

Focus: Chromium (VI) pollution and the development of innovative nanocomposite catalysts for its removal.

Problem Statement: Chromium Contamination

  • Chromium (VI) is a hazardous and carcinogenic pollutant commonly found in industrial wastewater.
  • It is difficult to remove by conventional water treatment methods, especially at low concentrations.
  • There is a pressing need for efficient, cost-effective, and environmentally friendly remediation technologies.

Research Objectives

  1. To synthesize Ni-ZnFe₂O₄ nanoparticles with enhanced properties for catalytic remediation.
  2. To investigate the effect of synthesis conditions on nanoparticle characteristics and catalytic activity.
  3. To evaluate the efficiency of Ni-ZnFe₂O₄ nanoparticles in reducing Cr(VI) ions in contaminated water.

Overall Research Aim:

To develop nanocomposite ferrites with high magnetic storage capacity, high electron storage capacity, and superior photocatalytic removal capability for chromium remediation.

Literature Review Highlights

Conventional Methods & Limitations

  • Methods: Membrane filtration, chemical precipitation, ion exchange.
  • Limitations: High cost, secondary waste, ineffective at low concentrations.

Magnetic Ferrites: A Promising Solution

  • High surface area, stability, and magnetic separability for reuse.
  • Excellent candidates for photocatalysis.

Synthesis Approaches

  • Various methods exist: Hydrothermal, combustion, sol-gel.
  • Co-precipitation chosen for this study: Simple, cost-effective, produces nano-sized particles.

Research Gaps

  • Optimization of the Zn/Ni ratio for maximum efficiency.
  • Long-term stability and recycling efficiency studies are needed.

Materials & Methodology

Synthesis Method

  • Co-precipitation synthesis of Ni-ZnFe₂O₄ nanoparticles.
  • Three sample compositions (S1–S3) with varied Ni/Zn ratios.
  • pH controlled precipitation (pH 10-11) using NaOH.
  • Final thermal treatment at 200°C.

Reagents Used

  • Ni(NO₃)₂·6H₂O, 99.52% (Sigma Aldrich)
  • Zn(NO₃)₂, 99.89% (Sigma Aldrich)
  • Fe(NO₃)₂, 97.5% (Sigma Aldrich)
  • NaOH (for pH adjustment)
  • Deionized water

Synthesis Procedure

  1. Dissolve Ni(NO₃)₂, Zn(NO₃)₂, and Fe(NO₃)₂ separately in deionized water.
  2. Combine solutions and stir vigorously at 700 rpm.
  3. Add 1M NaOH dropwise until a pH of 10-11 is reached to form a precipitate.
  4. Allow the precipitate to settle and filter 5 times at 2-hour intervals to remove impurities.
  5. Dry the precipitate in an oven at 200°C for 1 hour.
  6. Grind the dried powder and calcinate at 200°C for 1 hour to obtain the final nanocomposite.

Key Findings

  • Ni-ZnFe₂O₄ nanocomposites were successfully synthesized and confirmed by all analyses.
  • The optimal Zn/Ni ratio is critical for performance, with S3 exhibiting the best efficiency.
  • Achieved >87% Cr(VI) removal under optimal conditions (pH 3, 120 min).
  • A smaller particle size (~44 nm) correlated directly with higher photocatalytic efficiency.

Structural Achievements

Cubic spinel structure confirmed via XRD. Fe-O bonding verified by FTIR. Particle size control (44-64 nm) was demonstrated.

Practical Benefits

The nanocomposites are stable, magnetic, and reusable, offering a low-cost solution for industrial wastewater treatment.

Photocatalytic Mechanism

  1. Light Absorption: Ni-ZnFe₂O₄ absorbs visible light, generating electron-hole (e⁻/h⁺) pairs.
  2. Reduction of Cr(VI): Photogenerated electrons (e⁻) reduce toxic Cr⁶⁺ to the less toxic Cr³⁺.
    Cr⁶⁺ + 3e⁻ → Cr³⁺
  3. Radical Formation: Holes (h⁺) react with H₂O to form hydroxyl radicals (•OH). Electrons also react with O₂ to form superoxide radicals (O₂•⁻). These highly reactive species degrade organic pollutants.
    H₂O + h⁺ → •OH + H⁺

Applications and Significance

Industrial Wastewater Treatment

  • Textile industry (dye effluent)
  • Tannery waste (high chromium)
  • Electroplating (metal ion removal)

Potential for Multiple Contaminants

Effective for Chromium (VI), with potential for Lead (Pb) and Arsenic (As) removal.

Key Significance

  • A cost-effective alternative to conventional treatment methods.
  • Environmentally sustainable approach using visible light.
  • Contributes to UN Sustainable Development Goal #6: Clean Water and Sanitation.

Conclusions

Ni-ZnFe₂O₄ nanocomposites are efficient, scalable photocatalysts for Cr(VI) removal.

Structural and functional integrity were confirmed, and the best efficacy was achieved with a controlled Zn/Ni ratio and smaller particle size.

Key Achievement

Successfully developed a low-cost, reusable nanocatalyst for the efficient photocatalytic remediation of hazardous hexavalent chromium from wastewater.

Future Work & Recommendations

Research Directions

  • Investigate surface functionalization to enhance selective adsorption.
  • Further optimize the Zn/Ni ratio for maximum efficiency.
  • Conduct long-term stability and reusability studies (>10 cycles).
  • Test efficacy for other heavy metals and complex industrial effluents.

Practical Applications

  • Explore pilot-scale implementation for industrial use.
  • Develop integrated water treatment systems combining Ni-ZnFe₂O₄ with other technologies for comprehensive pollution control.
